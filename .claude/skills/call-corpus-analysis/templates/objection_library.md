# Objection Library (structure + how to build)

Built from real transcript quotes. Every wording below is a placeholder — replace with the actual
quotes your fan-out agents surfaced (Phase 3, section 3).

## 4 rebuttal principles
1. **Agree + pivot** ("I understand, that's why…") — not defense.
2. **Math to math** — answer a number with your number + full breakdown.
3. **Proof over promise** — "here's a person who does this" beats "we're reliable".
4. **Concrete next step** — "let me set up X tomorrow", not "think about it".

## Entry format


## Standard categories to cover (rename to your domain)
1. **Price / value** — "you pay too little", "competitor pays more", "I have X years, I want more"
2. **Documents / eligibility** — "waiting on my permit", "only have a visa", "unfinished paperwork"
3. **Location / logistics** — "don't want region A", "too far"
4. **B2B / upsell / format** — "I want a contractor/subcontractor deal", "turnkey by the unit"
5. **Trust / "I've been scammed"** — answer with 3-4 concrete proofs, not reassurance
6. **"Let me think / ask my spouse"** — pin a concrete next step (a 15-min call), offer to include the spouse
7. **"I'll find it myself" / skepticism** — quantify the time/cost you save them; offer a risk-reversal

## Worked example (price objection — math-to-math)
```
### "You pay too little — competitor offers more"
Heard in: price-mismatch basket (frequent)
❌ "That's just our rate, but it's net."
✅ "Got it — is that net or gross? With or without housing deducted? What do you actually take home
   at month-end right now? … OK. Our math: {{units}} × {{rate}} = {{gross}}, minus {{housing}},
   minus {{fuel}} = {{net}}. You're at {{their_net}} today — so we're ~{{delta}} more, plus
   {{insurance/tools}}. That's {{annual_delta}}/yr. Let's double-check I didn't miscount?"
```
